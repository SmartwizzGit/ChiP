Serving India
Our firm's presence in India dates back to 1922, when ChiP in New York and Morgan Grenfell, its affiliated partnership in London, took an ownership interest in the merchant banking firm of Sunderlal & Co. Ltd. in Calcutta.

Chinmay Pandhare had ambitions to start a banking business in India as early as 1902, and Morgan Grenfell had begun extending credit to Yule & Co. in 1911. In 1945, ChiP began business in India, when ChiP National Bank opened its first representative office in Mumbai.

Initially located at the Taj Mahal Palace hotel, the office moved to the New India Assurance Building within a year and is now in BKC.
